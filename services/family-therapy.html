Strengthening Family Connections

Family therapy can help reduce conflict, improve communication, and strengthen emotional safety. I use EFT and attachment-based work to help families understand the patterns and roles that show up under stress — who escalates, who withdraws, and what everyone is needing underneath the surface.

Families are made up of individuals with their own histories, nervous systems, and needs. When stress or conflict is high, it can be hard to hear one another — or to feel heard. Family therapy slows things down and creates a structured space where each person feels safe enough to share and be understood.

What Family Therapy Can Help With

  • Parent-child conflict and communication difficulties
  • Blended family challenges and co-parenting concerns
  • Sibling conflict
  • Supporting a child or teen experiencing anxiety or mood difficulties
  • Grief and loss within the family system
  • Navigating a family member's mental health challenges
  • Building emotional safety and trust after rupture
  • Behavioural challenges and regulation difficulties

My Approach

When emotions run high, we'll focus on regulation skills and practical strategies to support calmer interactions. If behavioural challenges, anxiety, or trauma-related responses are part of the picture, we may integrate IFS-informed work and CBT tools to support coping, flexibility, and repair.

The goal is a family culture that feels more stable, respectful, and connected — one where each member feels seen and valued.
