Culturally Safe Care

I welcome Indigenous clients and provide compassionate and decolonizing care. My approach is guided by Two-Eyed Seeing and recognizes the ongoing impact of colonization, systemic inequities, racism, grief, loss, and intergenerational trauma on mental health and relationships. My work will honour your identity, values, and community connections.

Therapy can be a place to slow down, reconnect with your inner wisdom, and find steadier ground — emotionally, relationally, and in your body. You are the expert on your own life and experience. My role is to walk alongside you, not ahead of you.

What We Can Work Through Together

We'll work in a way that supports calm, clarity, and self-trust, whether you're navigating:

  • Relationship stress and family dynamics
  • Trauma and intergenerational trauma
  • Grief and loss
  • Overwhelm and anxiety
  • Self-doubt and people-pleasing
  • Shutdown or hypervigilance
  • Identity, belonging, and cultural connection
  • The impact of systemic racism and discrimination

Therapy can help you strengthen boundaries, reconnect with your voice, and shift relational patterns.

My Therapeutic Approach

My work is trauma-informed and attachment-based, integrating:

  • Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) for relationship healing
  • Internal Family Systems (IFS) for trauma work and self-understanding
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT) for practical coping tools
  • Two-Eyed Seeing — honouring both Indigenous and Western knowledge systems
  • Nervous System Regulation — supporting your body's capacity to feel safe

Sessions are paced with care, choice, and collaboration. There is room for your story, your strengths, your grief, and your resilience.

NIHB Coverage

I accept Non-Insured Health Benefits (NIHB) for eligible First Nations and Inuit clients. Please reach out to confirm your coverage prior to booking.
